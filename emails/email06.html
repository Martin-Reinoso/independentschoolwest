A Critical Milestone

We are applying to officially register Rosewood College

Over the coming days, we will be taking one of the most important steps in the life of Rosewood College: formally applying to have the school registered with the Victorian government. Years of hard work has led the Families for Education team to this moment. The formal registration is the step that makes everything real.

What does this mean?

In Victoria, every school must be approved and registered by a government body called the VRQA (Victorian Registration and Qualifications Authority) before it can open. Think of it as the licence to operate. A successful application demonstrates that Rosewood College meets every requirement — from governance and curriculum to staffing and the wellbeing of students.

When will we know the outcome?

Our submission must be received by the VRQA before 30 June. Following a preliminary review, we hope to receive initial feedback in July. A successful preliminary outcome would mean we can officially begin the enrolment process for Rosewood College, as we continue working toward full registration and opening.

A Request for Your Prayers

We ask that you please pray for our submission — that God may guide the hearts of those who review it, and grant us the outcome that serves His purpose.

Rosary Pilgrimage

On Saturday 23 May, families gathered at Our Lady Ta' Pinu Shrine in Merrimu to pray the Rosary together — for the needs of our families and for the school. It was a moving morning of shared faith, followed by a simple picnic together, with children running around and families getting to know one another. These moments of prayer as a community are part of what will make Rosewood College something more than just a school.

Option One
01

The Living Tree

Growth, rootedness, and the quiet authority of a school that has always been there.

Concept Rationale

Rooted in
virtue.

The emblem builds on the traditional Australian girls' school oval, taking a confident step forward. At its heart, a flourishing tree rises with outstretched branches — a quiet metaphor for character formation, intellectual growth, and the lifelong fruit of a well-rooted education.

The motto Ad Summam Virtutem — "Toward the highest virtue" — arches above, framing the crest with academic purpose. Below, a single rose appears in subtle relief: an honoured nod to the school's name, refined enough to remain distinctive from neighbouring institutions.

The overall composition is warm, dignified, and unmistakably scholarly. It carries the weight of tradition without leaning on the past — a mark designed to belong to Rosewood for the next twenty-five years.

Option Two
02

The Heritage Rose

A traditional crest — refined, classical, and unapologetically rooted in academic heritage.

Concept Rationale

A name made
visible.

This composition takes the most direct route to the school's identity: the rose, plainly named, plainly drawn. The emblem honours the literal meaning of "Rosewood" with a single, upright bloom — confident, classical, and unmistakably feminine without ornamentation.

Twin laurel branches flank the rose in restrained gold, signalling academic distinction in the heraldic tradition of Australia's most established girls' schools. The motto Ad Summam Virtutem is set on a crimson banner below — a clear, formal anchor.

Where Option One favours metaphor, Option Two favours directness. It is the more traditional of the two — better suited to formal contexts, ceremonial use, and audiences who expect a school crest to look unmistakably like one.

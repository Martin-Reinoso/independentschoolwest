## Board of Directors

## Purpose

This dossier provides a concise summary of recent activities and immediate next steps for the Board of Directors.

## Executive Summary

Recent activity has focused on three main areas:

1. Community-building through the Family Picnic held on 26 April 2026.
2. Enrollment outreach and follow-up classification of prospective families.
3. Final preparation for the upcoming Online Information Session on 20 May 2026.

Together, these activities have helped strengthen community engagement, clarify likely enrollment demand, and build momentum toward the next major public milestone for the project.

## 1. Family Picnic

The Family Picnic was held on Sunday, 26 April 2026 at Frontier Park, Aintree from 1:00 p.m. to 3:00 p.m.

Attendance was strong, and the event appears to have been successful in strengthening relationships among interested families and reinforcing confidence in the project. Communications around the event were handled in two stages:

- A pre-event newsletter invitation was sent to families in advance.
- A post-event recap newsletter was later circulated, reflecting positively on the afternoon and its role in community-building.

The picnic helped give families a tangible sense of the culture and community that is being formed around the future school. Special thanks are due to Thomas for hosting and helping make the afternoon a warm and welcoming success.

## 2. Enrollment Outreach

The Enrollment Committee has now contacted all families on the current contact list by phone.

Following those conversations, substantial follow-up work was undertaken to classify families according to their current level of commitment and likely next steps. This process has helped sharpen the project's understanding of realistic near-term enrollment interest.

At present, the likely-enrollment group represents approximately 26 children. This updated information has already been passed to the Finance Committee so that cost assumptions and financial planning can be reassessed in light of a clearer enrollment outlook.

This work represents an important step forward in moving from general interest to better-qualified enrollment forecasting.

## 3. Online Information Session Preparation

Preparation for the Online Information Session is well underway, with a practice run scheduled for Sunday night ahead of the event.

The session will take place on Wednesday, 20 May 2026 from 8:30 p.m. to 9:30 p.m. via Google Meet. The evening is positioned as a significant milestone for the project and will include the public reveal of the school's name.

Families have already been invited privately, and an additional email communication is planned for Sunday to reinforce attendance and readiness. The published event agenda indicates that the session will cover:

- the school name announcement
- location and land development progress
- school fees
- ideals in teaching and spiritual formation
- the new website and expressions of interest
- open Q&A

This event is likely to be an important touchpoint for both existing interested families and newly engaged families, offering a structured opportunity to communicate progress and build confidence.

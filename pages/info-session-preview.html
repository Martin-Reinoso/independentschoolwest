School Name Reveal Recap

Rosewood College was announced on the night.

A major milestone in the life of Families for Education.

On Wednesday 20 May 2026, families and supporters gathered online for an important Information Night sharing progress across the project, including site, fees, formation, and the next steps toward opening in 2027. During the evening, the school name was publicly announced for the first time: Rosewood College.

What the evening covered

The Information Night brought together a number of the most important developments in the project so far. It gave families a clearer picture of how the school is taking shape and why this next stage matters so much.

School name announcement The project now has a public name: Rosewood College.
Location and master planning progress Families heard updates on the permanent site and the broader planning work underway for the school’s future home.
Fees and practical planning The night covered important practical questions, including fees and the planning assumptions shaping the path to opening.
Formation and educational vision The project’s distinctive commitment to family life, faith, character, and virtue remained central throughout the presentation.
Website, expressions of interest, and next steps The session also pointed families to the next practical steps for staying involved and registering interest.

Why the name Rosewood College?

From a spiritual perspective, the rose is a nod to Our Lady, while the wood recalls the wood of the Cross of Jesus.

From a human perspective, Rosewood evokes the image of a strong tree with deep roots. Deep roots give a tree strength, stability, and resilience. In the same way, the project seeks to help children grow in the human virtues that steady them through life, form character, and equip them to become a source of good for the world around them.

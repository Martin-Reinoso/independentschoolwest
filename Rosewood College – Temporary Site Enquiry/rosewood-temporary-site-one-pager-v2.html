Who We Are

About Families for Education

Families for Education is a non-profit organisation establishing Rosewood College — an independent co-ed Catholic primary school (Prep – Grade 5) in Melton, Victoria, planned to open in 2027 (subject to VRQA approval). While our permanent site is being finalised, we are seeking a temporary arrangement with a nearby local school to host our initial cohort of students.

Our Vision

Our Approach to Education

Our vision has developed through close engagement with the Parents for Education (PARED) schools, first established in Sydney and more recently in Melbourne’s south-east — schools with a strong track record of working closely with families in both academic and character formation.

At the heart of this approach is the recognition that parents are the primary educators of their children. This is expressed through a strong academic curriculum, a personalised mentoring system linking staff, students, and parents, and a deliberate focus on the formation of character and virtue.

Each student is paired with a mentor who meets regularly with both the student and their parents. The founding principal will be Dr Anne-Marie Irwin, founder of the SALT approach and a leading practitioner of the Spalding Method of structured literacy instruction in Australia.

Registration

VRQA Context

As part of ongoing discussions with the Victorian Registration and Qualifications Authority (VRQA), we have been advised that an approved site with a planning permit is required for registration.

As our permanent site is unlikely to have its permit finalised by the end of June 2026, the VRQA has recommended that we pursue a temporary arrangement with an existing school.

The Arrangement

What We Are Seeking

We are seeking to lease learning space for the 2027 school year — approximately 20–30 students (Prep to Grade 5) for up to 12 months, with a possible extension into 2028 if required.

We are flexible: existing classrooms, shared facilities, or demountable classrooms installed at our cost on available land. Our aim is a self-contained program with minimal disruption to your school community.
